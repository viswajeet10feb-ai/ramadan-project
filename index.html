06 — Design Decisions

Intentional choices behind the visual language

🌙
Purple + Gold Color System
Custom Ramadan palette — royal purple (#461780) + lantern gold — built in Photoshop with layered gradients. Gave the store a premium, festive feel distinct from everyday Flipkart listings.
🍚
Storytelling Before Products
"Legacy of Biryani" editorial strip intentionally before the product grid. Cultural context — Mughal era origins, Pakki vs Kacchi, UNESCO listing — primed purchase intent for the rice section.
✨
Animated Hero in After Effects
Staggered product fly-ins, crescent particle effects, and a gentle idle loop — all animated in After Effects and exported within Flipkart's app file size constraints.
🏷️
7-Brand Visual Hierarchy
Fortune & Tata at hero scale, co-sponsors in a bottom strip. "Brands in Spotlight" gave each co-sponsor a dedicated moment — visible but never competing with primary sponsors.

Key Learnings

Storytelling converts better than product density
The editorial strip added no products but increased downstream add-to-cart — cultural context primes purchase intent.
Nail brand hierarchy before design begins
Getting primary vs co-sponsor structure signed off upfront saved multiple revision rounds.
Sequencing beats complexity in animation
Simple, well-timed float-ins felt more premium than elaborate effects.
Every store needs a strong closing beat
The Biryani Corner CTA became one of the highest-click zones on the page.
