03 — Brief & Sponsors

Weaving 7 brands into one cultural moment

Ramadan is a high-intent grocery season on Flipkart — users actively stock up for Sehri and Iftar. The brief was a premium, culturally warm sponsored store that felt occasion-specific, not like a generic product page.

The challenge: integrating two major brand identities (Fortune & Tata) with five co-sponsors under a single Ramadan × Biryani theme, without any brand feeling secondary.

04 — Design Decisions

Intentional choices behind the visual language

🌙
Deep Navy + Gold Color System
Custom Ramadan palette (navy #1A3A4A + gold #C9922A) instead of Flipkart's default yellow. Built in Photoshop with layered gradients — gave the store a premium, festive feel distinct from everyday listings.
🍚
Storytelling Before Products
"Legacy of Biryani" editorial strip placed before the product grid. Cultural context — Mughal era origins, Pakki vs Kacchi, UNESCO listing — primed purchase intent for the rice section.
✨
Animated Hero in After Effects
Staggered product fly-ins, crescent particle effects, and a gentle idle loop — all animated in After Effects and exported within Flipkart's app file size constraints. Brand logos appear after product desire is established.
🏷️
7-Brand Visual Hierarchy
Fortune & Tata at hero scale, co-sponsors in a bottom strip. Deeper in the page, "Brands in Spotlight" gave each co-sponsor a dedicated moment — visible but never competing with primary sponsors.

Key Learnings

Storytelling converts better than product density
The editorial strip added no products but increased downstream add-to-cart — cultural context primes purchase intent.
Nail brand hierarchy before design begins
Getting primary vs co-sponsor structure signed off upfront saved multiple revision rounds.
Sequencing beats complexity in animation
Simple, well-timed product float-ins felt more premium than elaborate effects.
Every store needs a strong closing beat
The Biryani Corner CTA at the bottom became one of the highest-click zones.
